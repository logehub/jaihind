Our Legacy

From Refugee Relief to Educational Excellence

Soon after the partition of India in 1947, a group of Sindhi ladies displaced from East Pakistan started social work in Pune to provide education to Sindhi children. They formed the “Poona Refugee Relief Committee,” laying the foundation of today’s Trust.

Over the years, this initiative evolved into a formal educational institution committed to accessible, value-based education for all.

Name change:
In 1989, the “Poona Refugee Relief Committee” was officially renamed as Jai Hind Sindhu Education Trust through the Deputy Charity Commissioner, Poona Region, Poona.
